Empty states appear when there is **no data to show**: first visit, no search results, filters that exclude everything, or missing permissions. A strong empty state orients the user, sets expectations, and offers a **clear next action**—not just an illustration and a joke.

## Types of empty states

- **First use** — No records yet; emphasize onboarding and primary creation actions.
- **No results** — Search or filters returned nothing; suggest clearing filters or alternate queries.
- **Cleared content** — User deleted or archived items; confirm success and how to undo if applicable.
- **No permission** — Explain why content is hidden and how to request access—without exposing sensitive details.

## Illustrations

Use **simple, inclusive** artwork that matches brand tone. Avoid busy scenes that distract from the headline. Provide **alt text** when images convey meaning; decorative spots can use empty alt.

## Action prompts

Lead with a **short headline** (what is empty) and a **one-line description** (why or what to do next). Primary and secondary actions should mirror the most common user goals—often “Create X” and “Import” or “Learn more.”

## Layout

Center content vertically in the available region with consistent spacing from Foundations. On mobile, stack actions vertically full-width for touch targets.

| Element | Guidance |
| ------- | -------- |
| Headline | Sentence case; specific to the scenario |
| Body | One or two lines; no jargon |
| Actions | At most two primary paths; link tertiary help |
