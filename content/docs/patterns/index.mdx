Patterns describe **how components work together** in real user flows: validating forms, recovering from empty data, and guiding first-time users. They are not new primitives; they combine tokens, layout, and components under agreed rules so teams ship consistent UX.

## How patterns relate to components

A **component** answers “what building blocks exist?” A **pattern** answers “how do we structure this experience?” For example, Button and Text Field are components; “inline validation on blur with a summary on submit” is a pattern that specifies timing, messaging placement, and error anatomy.

## Adoption

Patterns may be **required** for certain product areas (e.g., admin tools) or **recommended** elsewhere. When a product diverges, document the rationale in your team’s decision log so accessibility and research can follow up.
