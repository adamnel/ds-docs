When to display assistive information, and how


General guidance (always visible)


Applies to part of the page

### 

Text directly in the page

Short explanations directly in the body of the page.

<imagePlaceholder imageId="content_info-help_general_part-of-page" />


Applies to an individual form field

### 

Form field hint

Display your message directly adjacent to the field itself.

Use this sparingly - it will be overwritten in the case of an error on that field. Most of the time you could do better with text directly in the page (see above).

<imagePlaceholder imageId="content_info-help_general_individual-field" />


Temporary guidance


Applies to the whole page

### 

Page-level alert

Use this sparingly - it will be overwritten in the case of an error on that field. Most of the time you could do better with text directly in the page (see above).

<imagePlaceholder imageId="content_info-help_temporary_full-page" />


Applies to part of a page, or a dialog

### 

Inline alert

An inline alert allows more specific messaging about a smaller region of a page, or a modal dialog or sheet.

<imagePlaceholder imageId="content_info-help_temporary_part-of-page" />


Applies to an individual form field

### 

Form field error message

Display your message directly adjacent to the field itself. In the case of errors and warnings, the form field also changes state to draw attention to itself.
