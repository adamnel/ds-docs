Accessibility is a **product requirement** for Covalent. We target **WCAG 2.2 Level AA** for customer-facing experiences unless a specific regulation requires stricter conformance. Meeting AA does not replace usability testing with assistive technology—automated checks catch only a fraction of issues.

## Checklist (baseline)

- **Keyboard** — All interactive controls reachable and operable without a mouse; visible focus states.
- **Color** — Contrast for text and UI components per level AA; information not conveyed by color alone.
- **Structure** — Semantic headings, landmarks (`header`, `nav`, `main`), and labels on form controls.
- **Motion** — Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; avoid auto-playing distracting animation.
- **Forms** — Errors identified, described, and associated with fields; suggestions where known.

## Testing tools

- **Automated:** axe DevTools, Lighthouse accessibility audits (supplemental).
- **Screen readers:** VoiceOver (Safari), NVDA (Firefox/Chrome on Windows), TalkBack on Android for mobile patterns.
- **Zoom:** Browser zoom to 200% and 400% for reflow checks on critical flows.

## Manual scenarios

Exercise **real tasks**: complete a form, sort a table with the keyboard, open and close dialogs, and navigate with rotor or landmarks in VoiceOver. Document known gaps with tickets and user impact.
