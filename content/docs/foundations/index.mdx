Foundations define the **visual and verbal substrate** of Covalent: how color encodes meaning, how type establishes hierarchy, and how spacing creates rhythm. Product teams should reach for **tokens** before hard-coding hex values or font sizes so themes (including dark mode) stay coherent when global decisions change.

## Why tokens matter

**Primitive tokens** capture raw values—brand blues, neutral grays, spacing units. **Semantic tokens** map those primitives to roles: `color.text.primary`, `color.surface.canvas`, `space.section.gap`. When marketing refreshes brand blue, semantic mappings update once; applications that consume tokens do not need a find-and-replace across every screen.

## Light and dark

Covalent treats **dark mode** as a first-class theme, not an inversion of light. Surfaces, borders, and text colors have dedicated semantic pairs so contrast stays within our targets without clipping or halo artifacts around inputs.
