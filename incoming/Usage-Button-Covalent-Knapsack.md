# Button / Usage

Buttons are interactive components that guide and inform a user’s interaction through an experience. They signal actions and provide clear visual cues to help users accomplish their goals with confidence and efficiency.

# 

When to use

Use button variants to prioritize actions: **Raised** for primary actions like "Submit," **Outlined** for secondary actions like "Cancel," and **Text** for less prominent, inline tasks. Each style ensures clarity and purpose.

**Variants**

**Purpose**

[Raised](/site/covalent/latest/pattern/button?templateId=web-components-raE1x4tYL) 

A primary button is used for the main action on a page or screen, such as "submit" or "save".

[Outline](/site/covalent/latest/pattern/button?templateId=web-components-raE1x4tYL) 

A secondary button is used for less critical actions or alternatives, like "cancel" or "back".

[Text](/site/covalent/latest/pattern/button?templateId=web-components-raE1x4tYL)

A tertiary button is used for less critical actions or alternatives, like "cancel" or "back". 

# 

Recommendations

Recommended practices ensure clear guidance, usability, accessibility, appropriate sizing, and user support.

Placement

![cv-button_placement](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-1878.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=j9CUTYZ3M7HTslo8tGaY4xzxJ5awLn189JveK5QpktzA4Iik4HuclWnHaBS-Bl-kDm6oDItPs3yqqZOZmvU1zWAEuxs-M1hwqPBKjFxZNWwtz4e7gJzNSlPdA0KN7NR-aSJ4bKSlgXVzQ~d45MjZ4rUOj-xIu7-eH2YPhJhJxaw8h853q3aCI77AVZ~TW72SmGEKG2wfSRgCOtp-jMUrKwE0G38defmzonEBC1v4e~X22dK9YXpbfRhxfKQsxcKyQLPSymUeXC-Ec31TKBlhSGOdFkMOfj7LD8p9HnxckuBXjhHsaqnqw8v9m4-yEXTzLCP7qV-ySao7jb4vb62zAQ__)

##### Do

**Buttons are organized by priority**, starting with the primary action on the far right (if applicable), followed by secondary actions and lesser functions like icon buttons.

##### Don't

Don’t place buttons in an order that confuses their priority or purpose, such as putting secondary actions ahead of the primary action.

![cv-button_in-footers](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-1901.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=lgtNeYG7Q4TBG1Wx5BA9EoiruEsIvABtsovWA-QvxN427yMCcSzHwRPQ8-aZL3FaCY1Qp50Yt4PiishQXtLMRdOTBSRbS15ETXeRP5VmtfObqTSkCg6CFuKh6QYggYLZdNH1qEvDKeESyni4-J3kcpVOPu1zI6pG2weeg~U8J7EcqtTiMLvL-6IxHiPdUcSvZThd4H-NjH9Chnqi~xHoCFgWiTGNwMfHm7i~Y90J21DPAL4ysnKBWUkQyugbvpwBISkDvjPcPScOnyz1ceyy85ll2TkAjmtmN8NtbdPh2AxuaA1-kpATaGrY88v3gyhZ-~MvDsjCKiUNWkeiLP-G5Q__)

##### Do

**In footers** (e.g., card, dialog, page, or sheet footers), align buttons to the right, placing destructive actions on the left to prevent accidental clicks.

##### Don't

Don’t place destructive actions on the right or mix button alignments, as this can lead to confusion or unintended actions.

Groupings & Types

![cv-button_action--primary](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-2111.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=VLH5BFrG8j5XZTwxGZKpdtgwEbkCZuqQYn1-kh9zurbQ9chel7wd-fcwTtbm2aTul4dsdn6fgDXEpt~A6kxtvrr1dJzfWLli9tfKk2aNDDLUoE5kRJdKP2qJyEPFV5eEuszC~wZPcmVQv3DlihA6yo3D3fWsn8CNX0VplW-2U1ozfSM2V~DLk2Xy2xJyw6biEiTn40bgFY3p1pH3UR4AmA5ENDBWfTNTasFfisVTOq1sAhw0vzZQODIXtlBK7E3jzpVQo-bQX1UAGo2t6hvZoZ1ZZIt03HKr7dAek6bt2xTlXvJVNC7qd5OvoAgfGYUtOr6IMOuABS1Q997vfdHlkw__)

##### Do

**Use one primary button per group of actions.** This ensures users clearly understand the most important action.

##### Don't

Don’t use multiple primary buttons in the same layout. This creates confusion about which action is most important.

![cv-button_action--primary](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-2111.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=VLH5BFrG8j5XZTwxGZKpdtgwEbkCZuqQYn1-kh9zurbQ9chel7wd-fcwTtbm2aTul4dsdn6fgDXEpt~A6kxtvrr1dJzfWLli9tfKk2aNDDLUoE5kRJdKP2qJyEPFV5eEuszC~wZPcmVQv3DlihA6yo3D3fWsn8CNX0VplW-2U1ozfSM2V~DLk2Xy2xJyw6biEiTn40bgFY3p1pH3UR4AmA5ENDBWfTNTasFfisVTOq1sAhw0vzZQODIXtlBK7E3jzpVQo-bQX1UAGo2t6hvZoZ1ZZIt03HKr7dAek6bt2xTlXvJVNC7qd5OvoAgfGYUtOr6IMOuABS1Q997vfdHlkw__)

##### Do

**Use secondary buttons (outlined) for less critical actions.** These should complement the primary button without overshadowing it.

##### Don't

Don’t place secondary buttons (outlined) to the right of the primary button. This breaks the priority flow.

![cv-button_action--destructive](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-2125.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=mAykp2KMVQlViWUrOBS2-OUEH8bovezsXcmGfiFwXUgpKdB3jx~-a7EjQ53wJ7xGD~PKPlIRJVGhDASZtQ5BHPbmoXi3~pQ4nYeDSHvkqsnpmwmwcObW90gmqWew6ghgtUZRcXHoZBWeoRNt84dUjEN3DJrGznnhSaJoZP68OH9O87GhpYvoHipxffDNkjuYyKltqzTW5RO7VQ7yT4K2mqnVyN5yuYKV-c0NjB2Xoa29N-QdeZoEqrshK1Q2bhkzO5WmH9HXXA0pczbiQOWg0bxjQ8qaYG5H-Ps4EFxC9IQ8tW0kKP7-o31Ja6v1A3XCzovTy4xkLKuSGtIMSx77BA__)

##### Do

Place the destructive button as the primary action (aligned to the right) only when it represents the main task, such as deleting critical data. Use the negative color to emphasize caution.

##### Don't

Use a destructive button as the primary action unless it is the primary task. Misusing this placement can confuse users and increase the risk of accidental actions.

![cv-button_action--destructive--not-pruimary](https://design-src.knapsack.cloud/figma/covalent/KvXVAmYdCVAS7hzkOa73Em/2261921067357318529/images/13746-2139.png?Expires=1768218327&Key-Pair-Id=K3CKBA7ZQV44SC&Signature=HX8bDcBdbrzPjgTy3Lv0bqtgltPIzYGFv1yLKykm8t19-g6RAoE4o45a-fAkIz5BHJQEPeECFosPR9cn6j56qyGupC7qPvF-B1Q3eZdFwhc5Ia57DuGUKB-Pz8N-HFvBbH-AKZfx02Zwn4B2zPV2OJqtusbMQWwSdmHQyi~vEwHQPv7F7UzmoNqj-awG0YwcLzq2Kra74AYTeem7jtOk2eORnsLhbk6~im8JwX4M067C2FdP01dfa-VJMY22usKOTSKLpkXpjV33txh6Q3qoSAs3edzlvLhn6QYyZKLJ352MbIiEaJAt0Az-lop8rGMdwSC0FTWwucdWCSLwM6OKCw__)

##### Do

In button groups when the destructive button is not the primary, use secondary (outlined) or tertiary (text) styles for destructive actions to keep the primary action visually dominant and clear.

##### Don't

Use the negative color for secondary or less critical actions, as it distracts from the primary action and creates unnecessary visual weight.

##### Do

*   Use clear and concise labels that directly communicate the action (e.g., “Submit” or “Delete”).
    
*   Highlight the primary action with a visually distinct button to establish clear priority.
    
*   Ensure buttons are properly spaced and sized for easy interaction, especially on mobile.
    

##### Don't

*   Don’t place destructive buttons too close to primary ones without clear separation to avoid accidental clicks.
    
*   Don’t rely solely on color to differentiate buttons; use outlines, icons, or other visual cues for accessibility.
    
*   Don’t overload interfaces with too many buttons, as this overwhelms users and complicates decision-making.
