# Clarity and efficiency / Writing mechanics

Write clean, clear, easy-to-consume content. Consistently. As a trusted guide, do the hard work of thinking, clarifying, and simplifying so users don’t have to. By following these guidelines, you'll create confidence-inspiring content users can count on.

These conventions support clarity, consistency, and the Teradata [voice](/site/covalent/nhYvdlKSIj/pages/voice-and-tone). Follow them not only to help users get their immediate jobs done but also to build credibility into our products and content.

## 

Bulleted lists

Introduce bulleted lists with a grammatically complete sentence. Don’t let the bullet points complete the sentence, because that can undermine translation accuracy. Languages have different syntax, so breaking a sentence might not work in other languages.

You can also introduce a bulleted list with a straightforward label or category.

##### Do

You can copy these database objects:

*   Hash indexes
    
*   Join indexes
    
*   Triggers
    

##### Don't

You can copy:

*   Hash indexes
    
*   Join indexes
    
*   Triggers
    

##### Do

You can copy data these ways:

*   Establish or recover systems for copying.
    
*   Copy a large volume of data from one system to another on a regular schedule.
    
*   Perform a partial copy of tables from a source system to a target system.
    

##### Don't

Data mover allows you to:

*   Establish or recover systems for copying.
    
*   Copy a large volume of data from one system to another on a regular schedule.
    
*   Perform a partial copy of tables from a source system to a target system.
    

##### Do

Main components:

*   Data Mover daemon
    
*   Data Mover agent
    
*   User interfaces
    

##### Don't

The main components are:

*   Data Mover daemon
    
*   Data Mover agent
    
*   User interfaces
    

Strive for parallelism among the bulleted items in a list:

*   Make each bulleted item one or more sentences, and end each sentence with a period. Or make each bulleted item a phrase or single word with no ending punctuation.
    
*   Avoid having a mix, where some bulleted items are one or more whole sentences and others are phrases or single words.
    
*   Use the same, or a similar, grammatical structure in each item, if possible.
    

##### Do

Include these user profile parameters:

*   Database user account
    
*   Temporary and spool space allocations
    
*   Default database
    

##### Don't

Include these user profile parameters:

*   Database user account
    
*   Allocate temporary and spool space.
    
*   You can assign a default database.

## 

Numbers (in prose)

In prose, spell out one through nine and use numerals for 10 and above.

Exceptions:

*   If you have a mix of numbers below 10 and 10 or above, use numerals.
    
*   Do not start a sentence with a numeral. Spell out the number or re-write the sentence.
    
*   Use numerals in measurements
    
*   Use numerals where space is limited or in visual representations of data, for example in tables or graphs.
    

For ordinals, spell out first through ninth, and use numerals for 10th and above.

  

##### Do

run these two queries

either 2 or 12 nodes

Ten compute groups might be enough.

10,000 units consumed

a system with 1 TB of storage

your second environment

##### Don't

run these 2 queries

either two or 12 nodes

10 compute groups might be enough.

ten thousand units consumed

a system with one TB of storage

your 2nd environment

## 

Numbers (on UIs)

Always use numerals on a UI, because they promote clarity and efficiency. Even at the beginning of a sentence, do not spell out numbers.

This saves space, makes the UI easy to scan and understand, and maintains visual consistency across languages.

##### Do

**5 days remaining**

##### Don't

**Five days remaining**

##### Do

You have 7 unread notifications.

##### Don't

You have seven unread notifications.

But don't write dates with numerals only. See [Date and time](/site/covalent/latest/pages/date-and-time).

##### Do

17 October 2024

17 October

October 2024

##### Don't

17/10/24

10/17

10/2024

## 

Possessives

Use possessives to add clarity, especially when you have multiple nouns or adjectives strung together.

##### Do

your virtualization software’s documentation

the compute group profile’s schedule change

##### Don't

your virtualization software documentation

the compute group profile schedule change

You can also use hyphens to add clarity. Although you can add prepositions and verbs for the same purpose, possessives and hyphens are more economical.

##### Do

the new-customer total-lifetime revenue prediction

##### Don't

the new customer total lifetime revenue prediction

the prediction of revenue to be generated over the total lifetime of new customers

## 

Serial comma

Use the serial comma. It goes before “and” or “or” in a list of three or more items. It helps distinguish between the last two items.

Compare these sentences:

*   I want to thank my parents, Juan, and Ana.
    
*   I want to thank my parents, Juan and Ana.
    

In the first sentence, Juan and Ana are not the subject's parents. The first sentence contains three parallel items, so it takes a serial comma.

In the second sentence, Juan and Ana are the subject's parents. The second sentence contains a noun with an appositive, not three parallel items, so it doesn't take a serial comma.

##### Do

You can schedule across time zones, troubleshoot an issue in the user's local time zone, or review usage in a specific area.

##### Don't

You can schedule across time zones, troubleshoot an issue in the user's local time zone or review usage in a specific area.

## 

Subject of a modifier

Make sure modifiers have clear subjects. A dangling modifier is a grammatical error that occurs if a modifying phrase does not have a clear subject to modify.

Example: After parking the car, the sky turned dark.

Because the subject of a modifier belongs close to the modifier, it sounds like the sky parked the car. The modifier’s subject is whoever parked the car, and that's missing.

Often dangling modifiers are caused by the passive voice.

##### Do

Before starting the service for the first time, synchronize the repositories.

Before starting the service for the first time, the database administrator synchronizes the repositories.

##### Don't

Before starting the service for the first time, the primary and secondary repositories need to be synchronized.

_(It sounds like the primary and secondary repositories will restart the service.)_
