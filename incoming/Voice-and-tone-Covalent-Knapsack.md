# Covalent / Voice and tone

Learn how to apply the Teradata voice and choose the right tone for what you're building, whether it's an experience, an in-product help article, or reference documentation.

# 

Voice is our identity

At Teradata, we have a brand voice that is courageous, confident, dynamic, and compassionate. Our voice needs to be the same across the brand and products because we're all working toward the same goal, which is to build connections with our customers and users.

While we keep a unified voice across Teradata, our focus in product content is to deliver on the promise made by marketing. That requires an experience-focused strategy for voice and tone so we can speak consistently and make connections with target audiences throughout the different stages of the user journey.

When someone is interacting with an experience in our product, they’re in conversation with us. Through the conversation, our product character and voice attributes define our relationship with the person. And needs and desires are the building blocks in the relationship.

# 

Teradata character and voice attributes

Think of the Teradata character as a **trusted guide** with these voice attributes:

## 

Simple and reliable

Our target audiences are business analysts, data scientists, data engineers, and IT administrators who work at large enterprises. They work with large amounts of data and an array of tools. We are a reliable solution to their data needs, making sure they always know what our product is about, what it does, and how it can help them.

Our users have technical expertise, but we don’t expect them to know how to use our product. So, when we speak, we keep it simple and easy to understand. To achieve that, we use words they’re familiar with, keep out Teradata-specific jargon, and structure the conversation.

## 

Confident and knowledgeable

As a leading data analytics and warehouse company, we are confident and knowledgeable. We’re determined to help our users achieve their goals, big and small. We deliver the right information to the right person at the right time. We’re dedicated to helping our users feel empowered to drive decision making with their data work. 

Because we stay informed about our users’ jobs to be done through continuous learning, we anticipate what they need next and talk about opportunities to help them achieve more.

## 

Empathetic and transparent

We understand handling data work is complex and can be frustrating. When errors or other undesirable results occur, we’re determined to help our users navigate the challenges with empathy and transparency.

Whether it’s a system or user error, our goal is to support the user every step of the way. This means we sweat the details to identify the most helpful information and communicate it clearly and simply with a supportive tone.

# 

A framework that creates a human-like relationship

We’re not just designing screens and instructions. We’re creating a human-like relationship. It means you need to show up. You’re also a character in the user’s story. It also means declaring your desires in the relationship. Showing up in the relationship and connecting with them is not optional. _It’s essential._

This framework of needs and desires is meant to help you deeply understand our users and how you show up beyond the adjectives that describe your character:

**Product vision**

To deliver exceptional user experiences through whatever platform our customers choose to use

**Goal for voice and tone**

Go beyond the complexity to create a human-like relationship

**Building blocks in the relationship**

**User needs**

  

Jobs to be done and Teradata-specific tasks

**User desires**  
  

What they want beyond the functional experience

**Teradata character's desires**

  

The way we show up in the relationship

1.  I need you to guide me through each task.
    
2.  I need you to connect me to people and data sources quickly and effectively
    
3.  I need you to speak simple, familiar, and relevant language
    
4.  I need to maintain control of my data and its quality and governance.
    
5.  I need you to help me get unstuck when I encounter errors.
    

1.  I want to feel confident and accomplished with my data work.
    
2.  I want you to recognize my sense of purpose, motivation, excitement, and dedication in driving meaningful outcomes with data endeavors.
    
3.  I want you to treat me like a smart professional.
    
4.  I want you to make it easy for me to meet quality, security, and regulatory guidelines.
    
5.  I want to feel supported.
    

1.  I want to provide the easiest paths to get a job done.
    
2.  I want to provide cohesive experiences from one context to another.
    
3.  I want to foster trust, confidence, and transparency in decision making.
    
4.  I want them to know we have their backs.
    
5.  I want to provide easy ways to get out of issues without the user getting frustrated.
    

  

In essence, I want to let them know we focus on creating value and delivering delightful solutions.

# 

Voice principles

Voice principles are meant to help you embody the voice attributes and behaviors of the trusted guide, the Teradata character.

## 

It's about them, not us

Teradata exists for our customers and users. We’ll know we’ve done a good job when they appreciate us for who we are, and they trust us because they can see themselves in our product. This means we design for moments of truth. Those are the times when our users are most receptive and when we’ll have the most impact on their experience and their perception of Teradata.

##### Do

Your work on the data pipeline has really paid off.

Amazing job on the optimization. Processing time has gone down from 5 hours to 3 hours, and resource consumption down by 30%.

##### Don't

Congrats on the pipeline success! We at Teradata are always happy to help you streamline your work.

##### Do

Your model is live.

It’s already showing a 5% increase in user engagement. We can’t wait to see what comes next!

\[Button\] Monitor performance

##### Don't

It's a huge success! Isn’t deploying models in VantageCloud Lake amazing?

\[Button\] Monitor performance

A moment of truth calls for specific details such as numbers or examples that reflect meaningful business results that are worth recognition. Avoid broad statements such as “streamline your work,” “pipeline success,” “huge success,” and tooting our own horn.

##### Do

You’re all done with your trial setup

To help you get started with VantageCloud Lake, we provide all of our resources under **Learn** to help you and your team succeed every step of the way.

\[Button\] View learning resources

##### Don't

Way to go!

You completed the steps to set up your trial. To help you get started with VantageCloud Lake, we provide all of our resources under **Learn** to help you and your team succeed every step of the way.

\[Button\] View learning resources

A Teradata task can be more onerous than the user would prefer. Completing it might feel like a major accomplishment. But drawing too much attention to the work’s completion might reinforce its difficulty.

A congratulatory voice in this case might come across as “You survived installing this difficult thing in Teradata!” Which runs counter to our desire to create value, not wrestling matches. 

Oftentimes, tasks are means to the user's end goals (the moments of truth). Instead of celebrations, let them know they've completed a task and guide them to the next thing.   

## 

Focus on the jobs to be done

Speak about the user’s jobs to be done by using relatable and familiar language to help them achieve their goals.

##### Do

Give your team access

Create the first database admin who can then add additional roles and permissions to help everyone do their data work.

##### Don't

Create a database admin

Use the credentials you set up previously to create a database admin.

When introducing a workflow that may not be familiar to the audience, speak about the job to be done before introducing the Teradata task. In other words, give them a reason to care about the actions you want them to take.

##### Do

Flows

Move data from an external object store to a table in a VantageCloud Lake environment. Load the data once to begin, load it repeatedly on a schedule, or stream it continuously and use the data as it arrives.

##### Don't

Flow

The Flow service supplies data to VantageCloud Lake for a single initial load, scheduled loads, or continuous loads.

Lead with the user's desired outcome, not technical details that enable that outcome. Determining what users don't need, or when they truly need something on their path to job completion, is just as important as anticipating what they need. As a trusted guide, we speak immediately to user goals.

##### Do

From the menu, here’s what you can do: 

*   Monitor queries
    
*   Add users
    
*   Load data
    

##### Don't

Use this menu to access the primary features, including:

*   Monitoring queries
    
*   Adding users
    
*   Loading data
    

When you’re introducing a user interface, speak to user needs more than interface design. Avoid the word “feature.” Instead, speak of what they can accomplish, not what they can access.

## 

Speak simply

Our users are busy. Teradata is one of the many tools in their organization’s data ecosystem. Speak in a simple, clear, and straightforward way that helps them move forward.  

##### Do

Create a database admin

##### Don't

What's next?

You’ve set up an environment. Now it’s a good time to create database admins.

##### Do

A realm is a way to group users for authentication purposes.

##### Don't

What is a realm?

A realm is a way to group users for authentication purposes.

Omit unnecessary words. A rhetorical question such as “What’s XYZ?” asks the user to think. This runs counter to our desire to provide and advise in the easiest way possible.

![](https://knapsack.imgix.net/site/covalent/whatsnextexample-lg51fakgmypng "An example for the principle "Speak simply"")

##### Do

What's next

Create an OAuth app to allow authentication between AI Unlimited and your Git provider account.

When you guide the user to accomplish a goal through a set of tasks, you can give them a sense of what to expect next using “What’s next” _without the question mark._ This can provide an engaging end to the storytelling. It can be a simple and helpful transition from one context to the next.

##### Do

Create the root user for your trial environment.

##### Don't

Create a DBC user so you can create a database admin user for your trial.

Teradata has received plenty of feedback that our users don’t understand our internal jargon such as “DBC.” Find ways to remove the jargon, or minimize the impact of jargon if faced with technical constraints. The content design solution here is meant to bridge the gap by using commonly understood industry terms, in this case, “root user,” and explain the task in a way that's easy to understand.
