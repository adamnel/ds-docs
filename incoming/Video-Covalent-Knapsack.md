# Covalent / Video

You can use in-product video to help people understand a product or feature. Use these guidelines to make a video that can be helpful and timely.

**The content team does not currently support video. These guidelines are important considerations for now and guidance for how Teradata might support video in the future.**

# 

Considerations before you make a video

## 

Should we have a video?

Ideally, the product or feature experience shouldn’t need video content. Don’t use video to explain an unnecessarily complicated product or feature. Instead, work with your design and content partners to create a better product experience.

**Here's how you can decide when to use video in product:**

*   To give a brief high-level overview of a product or feature
    
*   To show the user how to use a product or feature to get to the outcome quickly
    
*   To show use cases of a product or feature. This can be especially useful in first-time experience or documentation.
    

## 

Have a strategy for sustaining video quality

Video content quickly goes out of date, as Teradata makes product and design changes regularly. It's also possible that updates are needed outside development cycles. Have a plan for maintaining and updating the video. The plan should include working with your content and design partners to monitor all video content and revise or remove as needed.

## 

Measure success of the video

How will you be tracking the usage and effectiveness of the video? Where and how often are you reporting and reviewing this data? Video is a costly and time-consuming medium. We need metrics to determine how or if we invest in this method of communication in our product.

# 

Content guidelines for creating a video

## 

Follow the Teradata brand guidelines

Use the Inter font for all captions and lower thirds as well as all other text that appears on screen. To learn more details, go to [Brand Resources](https://teradata.seismic.com/app#/doccenter/1bbd33d9-2b54-49c6-8ec3-ed5c6476c417/doc/%252Fdd8401e4c1-b845-177b-4658-8a724a8f4b22%252Fdd92e50d90-1686-51e9-967b-40de6345998d%252Flfaa938b98-3752-4869-b1da-0219a282d192//?mode=view) (employees access only), then select the Teradata Brand Guidelines. In the brand guidelines, see the video section starting on page 50.  

## 

Identify a place to show the video

A video can be placed in the help window, on the main UI, in a modal dialog, and more. Each comes with size restrictions. [Work with the content team](https://forms.office.com/pages/responsepage.aspx?id=qstRkWv8SE-Ju4xKcZghOORK7hViMKBHnzNFpaIK4pZUQlZQQVRYUTI5UEZDNE5LVFY0TDBEVFVETi4u&route=shorturl) to identify the best place to show the video in product. 

## 

Shorter is better

Limit a video to no more than 2 minutes.

## 

Make it accessible

Make sure a video is captioned so it's accessible for all people. 

## 

Make it translatable

Contact [the translation team](mailto:yoichi.ikeda@teradata.com?subject=Video%20translation%20question) to incorporate translation guidelines into your video content.

## 

Provide a transcript 

If your video is a step-by-step tutorial, consider transcribing the content. [Work with the content team](https://forms.office.com/pages/responsepage.aspx?id=qstRkWv8SE-Ju4xKcZghOORK7hViMKBHnzNFpaIK4pZUQlZQQVRYUTI5UEZDNE5LVFY0TDBEVFVETi4u&route=shorturl) to ensure a transcript is properly designed and presented along with the video.
